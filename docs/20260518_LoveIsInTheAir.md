#### Quick Overview of Jiddu Krishnamurti (1895–1986)
Jiddu Krishnamurti was an Indian-born philosopher, speaker, and spiritual teacher who became one of the most influential independent thinkers of the 20th century. Discovered as a boy by the Theosophical Society and groomed to become the “World Teacher,” he famously dissolved that organisation’s expectations in 1929, rejecting all guru status, authority, and organised religion.

For the next ~57 years he travelled the world speaking to large audiences, emphasising **psychological freedom**, self-inquiry, and direct perception of truth without intermediaries. Core teachings revolve around:
- Observing the mind without judgement
- Ending psychological time (past/future projections)
- Understanding fear, conditioning, and the self (“the observer is the observed”)
- Living in complete awareness and compassion

He left no followers or formal religion — only his talks, books, and foundations dedicated to preserving the teachings in their pure, non-dogmatic form.
