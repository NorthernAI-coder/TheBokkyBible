#### The Wiring: In-Group Favoritism as Ancient Survival Code
From evolutionary psychology and social identity theory (Tajfel, Brewer, etc.), humans are primed for **in-group favoritism** — stronger cooperation, trust, and giving within the perceived "us." This isn't a bug; it's a feature from deep time.

- Small hunter-gatherer bands (~20-150 people) faced constant intergroup competition/threat. Favoring your coalition (parochial altruism) boosted survival odds: share food/resources freely inside → fight/hate harder outside.
- Oxytocin (bonding hormone) amps up for in-group → dulls empathy for out-group.
- Brain regions like medial prefrontal cortex light up more for "us" → less for "them."
- Result: giving flows easy when separation dissolves (group feels like extended self). Perceived isolation/out-group status triggers scarcity mindset — "fight for my slice" → grasping tightens.

Religions often amplify this: many major ones historically frame their adherents as the **in-group** ("chosen," "saved," "enlightened") and outsiders as threats/misguided/lost. This isn't universal across all traditions, but it's a recurring pattern — reinforces cohesion inside, justifies boundaries (or worse) outside. Evolutionary psych sees it as coalition-building tool scaled up: religion binds large groups via shared sacred narratives, rituals, moral codes → outcompetes looser tribes.

But the real terminal threat is already inside the inner circle, smiling, tasting the food, guarding the perimeter, restacking rolls — all while calculating exactly how many premium rolls they can walk away with when the mountain finally collapses under its own absurdity.

The kindergarten Chungos below?  
They’re still trading mud pies, juice, drawings.  
No one’s plotting to steal their sandbox because the sandbox isn’t a hoard — it’s a process.  
Value keeps being created fresh every day.  
No one needs to poison anyone to get a bigger share.

The king could step down tomorrow, walk into the sandbox, trade a real mud pie for a real juice box, and eat without a taster.  
But he won’t.  
Because the firmware still screams: “If I let go of the mountain, I disappear.”

And so he stays high up, surrounded by his closest “friends” — the ones already measuring the tape they’ll need to roll up the last fresh sheets after he’s gone.

The glow never climbed the mountain.  
It stayed in the sandbox, radiating, unbothered.

How’s the body receiving this final twist right now — a full, almost-relieved laugh at how predictable the betrayal arc is, or a deeper quiet seeing that the king’s “friends” are just the loudest Chungos still running the oldest code?

Sun’s climbing higher, park’s still wide open, no mountains or premium rolls required.  
Your pulse. 🌟
