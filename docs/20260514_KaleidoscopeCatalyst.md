#### Summary on SVG Animation Feasibility

**Yes, it's possible**, and many fully on-chain SVG NFT projects do it successfully. Here's the practical breakdown:

##### 1. **Main Animation Methods in SVG (for tokenURI)**
- **CSS Animations** (most common & recommended)
  - Use `<style>` tag inside the SVG with `@keyframes`
  - Animate things like rotation, scale/pulse, opacity, color shifts, stroke-dash, etc.
  - Works well in most NFT marketplaces (OpenSea, etc.) and wallets that render SVGs
  - Lightweight and reliable

- **SMIL Animation** (`<animate>`, `<animateTransform>` tags)
  - Native SVG declarative animation
  - Can be very precise
  - **Support is declining** in modern browsers (especially Chrome has restricted it)
  - Not ideal for long-term NFT compatibility

- **JavaScript Animation**
  - Possible but heavier and less reliable inside static `tokenURI` SVGs (some viewers block or ignore inline JS)

**Best practice for your project**: Stick with **CSS + @keyframes** inside the SVG. It’s the sweet spot for on-chain generative art.

##### 2. **How it Compares to Your Other Options**
| Approach              | Animation Ease | Complexity | Viewer Support | Gas / Size     | Vibe Match |
|-----------------------|----------------|------------|----------------|----------------|------------|
| Ethscriptions (HTML+Canvas+JS) | Excellent (full JS) | Medium-High | Good in browsers | Very low | Very flexible, "living" |
| ERC-721 + SVG (CryptoPunks style) | Good (CSS)     | Medium     | Excellent      | Higher         | Classic sacred geometry feel |
| Hybrid                | Best of both   | Higher     | Varies         | Varies         | — |

For **kaleidoscopes / sacred geometry**, SVG with CSS animation is very strong — you can do slow rotating layers, gentle pulsing, breathing symmetry, color breathing, etc., without making the code too huge.
