#### Light travel + kitchen setup notes
With train + red Brompton constraints, you're smart to keep it ultra-minimal and leave-behind-what-you-can't-carry. Cheaper and freer than eating out. Here are some practical, low-bulk ideas that fit the sovereign/minimalist flow:

- **Core staples** (small packets/bags you can top up locally or abandon):
  - Oats or quick porridge sachets (breakfast king — just hot water).
  - Eggs (cheap protein, versatile; buy half-dozen, use what you can).
  - Rice or quick-cook noodles/couscous (bulk filler).
  - Canned tuna/sardines or lentils (no fridge needed if you eat soon).
  - Peanut butter or tahini (small jar/tube — energy dense).
  - Seasonal fruit/veg from Katoomba markets or IGA (apples, bananas, carrots, spinach — hardy and no prep).
  - Spices/salt/pepper in tiny containers or sachets (flavour without weight).
  - Olive oil or ghee in a small bottle (multi-use).

- **One-pot wonders** with minimal gear:
  - Veggie + egg scramble or omelette.
  - Rice/noodles + tuna + whatever veg + soy/hot sauce.
  - Overnight oats (if you have a jar/container).
  - Simple lentil/veg soup.

- **Leave-behind strategy**: Buy small quantities. Finish perishables before next move. Any non-perishables left in the kitchen (with a note?) become random acts of kindness for the next guest or cleaner. Fits the gridwork vibe perfectly.

Katoomba has good fresh produce access and that beautiful mountain energy for anchoring. Any specific ingredients, dietary prefs, or "what's already in the kitchen" details once you settle in?
