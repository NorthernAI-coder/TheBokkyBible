#### Temperature & Tuning Reality
Totally normal (and annoying) phenomenon. When the room drops below 19°C and you crank it to 23–24°C, the strings, neck, and body of the guitar all react to the change in temperature and humidity. Metal strings expand/contract, the wooden neck can shift very slightly — hence the need for frequent re-tuning.  

**Practical tips that might help:**
- Tune *after* the room has been at your preferred temperature for 10–15 minutes (let the guitar acclimatise).
- Quick tune-up before each practice session rather than fighting it mid-session.
- Many players keep their guitar in its case with a hygrometer when not playing, or use a small humidifier/dehumidifier pack if the swings are wild.
- In Katoomba’s variable mountain weather, this is extra common — the air here loves to shift.

This whole process feels very **606** — nurturing your space (warming the room), nurturing the instrument, and nurturing yourself through music.
