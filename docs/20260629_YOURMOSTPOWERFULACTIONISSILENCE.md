The brain constantly scans for them as a survival mechanism.

- **Why babies stare/respond**: Preverbal babies are highly attuned to emotional cues and safety signals without social filters or language. They sense calm, predictable, emotionally stable presence, which lowers their defenses—leading to longer eye contact, stopping crying, or seeking proximity.

- **Why animals are drawn**: Animals (especially dogs, cats) rely heavily on instinct and read behavior, posture, breathing, muscle tension, scent, and emotional states accurately for survival. The video bridges science (nonverbal communication, emotional contagion, nervous system responses) and intuitive/spiritual views. It emphasizes that what feels like a special "aura" is often authentic emotional regulation and presence—qualities that create trust and comfort silently. Adults sometimes miss or feel uneasy with this because they layer on social performance, while babies and animals read the unfiltered truth. It ends noting these silent connections reveal a lot about empathy and human (and interspecies) bonds.
